# The Marshall Principle

Artificial intelligence may assist human decision-making.

Responsibility always remains with humans.

This principle anchors the governance philosophy of the Marshall Standard.

All framework design decisions must remain consistent with this principle.

---

# Framework Overview

The Marshall AI Governance Standard is a lightweight governance framework designed to help organizations safely adopt AI tools while maintaining clear human accountability.

The framework focuses on practical governance behaviors rather than complex compliance structures.

The goal is to give organizations visibility into AI use, establish responsibility boundaries, and reduce risk exposure.

---

# Core Governance Domains

The framework evaluates governance readiness across five domains.

### Governance Authority
Organizations must clearly identify who is responsible for AI usage policy and oversight.

### AI Visibility
Organizations must understand where and how AI tools are being used.

### Data Governance
Organizations must ensure that sensitive or proprietary data is not improperly exposed to AI systems.

### Human Oversight
Organizations must maintain human responsibility for decisions assisted by AI systems.

### Vendor Governance
Organizations must understand the external AI services they rely on and manage associated risks.
