---

## The Marshall Principle

Artificial intelligence may assist human decision-making, but responsibility always remains with humans.

This principle governs the entire MAGRS framework.

---

## AI Governance Readiness Definition

An organization is AI Governance Ready when it has documented its use of artificial intelligence, established boundaries for its use, and assigned human responsibility for AI-assisted outcomes.

### Required Evidence of Readiness

The following artifacts represent the minimum evidence required to demonstrate AI Governance Readiness:

- AI Usage Register  
- AI Boundaries Statement  
- AI Responsibility Declaration

## Participation Lifecycle

Awareness  
→ Readiness Assessment  
→ Readiness Activation ($85)  
→ Participation ($295 annual)  
→ Quarterly Reporting  
→ Annual Renewal  

Participation begins on the first day of the next calendar quarter after activation.

---

## Participation Status

Applicant  
Participating Organization – Level 1  
Continuing Organization – Level 2  

Additional levels may be introduced as the framework evolves.

---

## Reporting Discipline

Participating organizations submit a readiness report each quarter confirming:

- AI usage changes  
- leadership review of AI-assisted work  
- any concerns or incidents  

Reports are due at quarter end with a 10-day grace period.

Failure to report results in temporary suspension of participation credentials until reporting is brought current.

---

## Credential System

Participating organizations may receive:

- MAGRS participation badge  
- framed certificate  
- organization identifier  
- verification page  

Credentials remain valid only while participation status is active.

---

## Confidentiality Model

Participation in MAGRS is confidential by default.

Organizations may choose to:

- keep participation private  
- allow verification when requested  
- publicly display participation credentials  

MAGRS does not publish a public registry of participating organizations.
