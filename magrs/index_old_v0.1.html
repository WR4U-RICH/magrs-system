Marshall AI Governance Readiness Standard

A practical operational framework for responsible governance of artificial intelligence systems.

Concept

The Marshall AI Governance Readiness Standard is a named operational framework for introducing responsible governance practices for artificial intelligence systems inside organizations.

The framework was developed by Richard Marshall to address the growing operational gap between the rapid adoption of artificial intelligence tools and the slower development of governance structures needed to oversee their use.

Its purpose is to help organizations introduce visibility, accountability, and operational discipline for artificial intelligence systems before governance failures emerge.

Definition

The Marshall AI Governance Readiness Standard is a practical operational framework designed to help organizations introduce responsible governance practices for artificial intelligence systems.

Rather than focusing on complex regulatory compliance or abstract ethical principles, the framework emphasizes operational discipline: visibility into artificial intelligence usage, defined data boundaries, accountable decision ownership, and structured governance practices that evolve alongside technology adoption.

Why Governance Is Necessary

Artificial intelligence is entering organizations through everyday productivity tools, software platforms, and employee experimentation.

In many cases this adoption occurs before leadership establishes clear governance structures for responsible oversight.

Without deliberate governance, organizations may experience operational risks such as sensitive data exposure, unclear accountability for AI-assisted decisions, and uncontrolled expansion of AI tools across teams and departments.

The Marshall AI Governance Readiness Standard helps organizations recognize these risks early and introduce governance practices before problems appear.

The Adoption Model

The framework follows a simple operational loop:

Learn the framework.
Evaluate readiness.
Introduce governance practices.
Operate with accountable oversight.

Organizations typically begin by running a short readiness assessment designed to identify common governance blindspots in early AI adoption.

Participation

Organizations applying the framework may identify themselves as participating organizations in the Marshall AI Governance Readiness Standard.

Participation signals that the organization is actively working to introduce responsible governance practices for artificial intelligence systems.

Participation represents engagement with the framework rather than certification of perfect compliance.
